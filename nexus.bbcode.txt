[size=4][color=#00ffff]OVERVIEW[/color][/size]
Adds multi-ring support for the player, followers, and NPCs, so they can wear up to 10 rings at once, one on each finger.


[size=4][color=#00ffff]WHY ANOTHER MULTI-RING MOD?[/color][/size]
I wanted multi-ring support that feels like it fits Skyrim's normal equip system, with the normal [b]Equip[/b] and [b]Left Equip[/b] actions, inventory and favorites menu support, and no extra items or other workarounds just to manage it.

I also wanted it to be lightweight and low maintenance. You should not need a pile of dependencies just to wear extra rings, and if a ring is set up like a normal ring (see the Compatibility section for details), it should work without patches for every new ring mod, model replacer, or retexture.


[size=4][color=#00ffff]CONTROLS[/color][/size]
Rings now work like any other one-handed equip item, with full keyboard/mouse and controller support.
[list]
[*][b]Equip[/b]: Equips the ring on your right index finger.[/*]
[*][b]Left Equip[/b]: Equips the ring on your left index finger.[/*]
[/list]

[img]https://i.imgur.com/lxOKGsl.png[/img]

To pick a different finger, hold the configured [b]Finger select modifier[/b] while using [b]Equip[/b] or [b]Left Equip[/b]. You can also enable [b]Always choose finger[/b] to open the finger menu every time.

[img]https://i.imgur.com/0ZAE8fX.png[/img]

In the inventory item list, rings equipped on non-index fingers get a suffix after the item name, like [b]Gold Ring (L Middle)[/b] or [b]Gold Ring (L Middle, R Pinky)[/b]. This is only shown on the inventory row. It does not rename the ring itself.


[size=4][color=#00ffff]CONFIGURATION[/color][/size]
You can edit the INI directly:

[quote]Data\MCM\Settings\LeftHandRingsSKSE.ini[/quote]

Or use the optional MCM addon to change the settings in-game.

[list]
[*][b]Extra ring mode[/b]: Lets you choose between functional extra rings (keep enchantments and scripts) or cosmetic extra rings (only show the ring model).[/*]
[*][b]Enchantment strength[/b]: Lets you balance ring enchantment strength. Only equipped rings with at least one non-zero-magnitude enchantment effect are counted. You can keep full strength, use a fixed percentage, or split the total strength between counted rings.[/*]
[*][b]Always choose finger[/b]: Makes Equip and Left Equip open the finger selection menu without having to hold a modifier key.[/*]
[*][b]Respect UnequipAll[/b]: Clears extra rings when UnequipAll (papyrus function and console command supported) runs, then restores them after race transformations like werewolf or vampire lord.[/*]
[*][b]Virtual Slots[/b]: Lets you choose which finger is equippable individually, you can make it so that only the left-hand index finger and the right-hand index finger are enabled if you wish.[/*]
[*][b]NPC support[/b]: Enables virtual ring support for followers and other NPCs. NPCs can also be set to prefer The Bond of Matrimony on the left-hand ring finger.[/*]
[/list]

[size=4][color=#00ffff]FOLLOWERS AND NPCS[/color][/size]
If NPC support is enabled, followers and other NPCs can wear extra rings from their inventory. This follows vanilla's auto-equip mechanic: give them rings, and the auto-equip system decides what they wear. For now, NPCs prefer higher-value rings, with one-finger rings favored over multi-finger rings. I might expand this later with better customization, like combat-style presets.

Manual ring assignment for followers and NPCs is outside the scope of this mod.

Distributing rings to NPCs is also outside the scope of this mod. A [b]Left Hand Rings SKSE - NPC Showcase SPID[/b] file is available in the Miscellaneous section of the Files tab as an example.

[size=4][color=#00ffff]COMPATIBILITY[/color][/size]
Normal rings should work without patches. Earrings, piercings, bracelets, and mixed accessories are ignored as rings, even if they use body slot 36 or the ClothingRing keyword.

[list]
[*][b]Vanilla, DLC, and CC rings[/b] - Supported.[/*]
[*][b]Mod-added rings using body slot 36 (Ring)[/b]: Supported if the wearable model contains only ring/finger geometry.[/*]
[*][b]Mod-added rings using another body slot[/b]: Supported if they use the vanilla ClothingRing keyword and the wearable model contains only ring/finger geometry.[/*]
[/list]

[b]Special cases[/b]
[list]
[*][b]Frostmoon Pack rings[/b]: Each different Frostmoon ring can provide its werewolf bonus at the same time. Extra copies of the same Frostmoon ring can still be worn, but they are cosmetic only.[/*]
[/list]

[list]
[*]Compatible with [url=https://www.nexusmods.com/skyrimspecialedition/mods/104215]The New Gentleman[/url] 4.1.0+. Older versions can crash with this mod.[/*]
[*]If you use [url=https://www.nexusmods.com/skyrimspecialedition/mods/33414]VR Menu Mouse Fix[/url], the Favorites menu in VR does not currently allow left-equipping rings. The inventory menu works fine though.[/*]
[/list]


[size=4][color=#00ffff]UNINSTALLATION[/color][/size]
[list]
[*]Unequip any extra rings you have equipped.[/*]
[*]Save the game. If you used NPC support, disable it in the MCM and close the menu before saving.[/*]
[*]Remove the mod.[/*]
[/list]

Avoid removing it mid-playthrough without unequipping your extra rings first, especially if the rings have scripts attached.
